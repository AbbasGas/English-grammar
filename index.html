  • THIS / THAT

  • This, that, these and those are demonstrative adjectives that express objects in the distance.

    We use this - that for singular.

  • We use these - those for plural.

  • The adjectives this, that, these and those have the same use that in Spanish.

  • THIS: singular

  • Usamos el this para hacer referencia a objetos, personas, situaciones, acciones, o lugares que se encuentran técnicamente muy cercanas a nosotros. Usamos el adjetivo this para afirmar, negar o hacer preguntas. El adjetivo this no distingue entre género masculino o femenino.

  • AFIRMATIVE

    Las oraciones afirmativas con this indican la existencia de un objeto, acción, persona o lugar que se encuentra técnicamente “junto a nosotros”.

    This tree is beautiful

    This beach is very popular

    This exam looks very difficult.

  • NEGATIVE

    La estructura del this en oraciones negativas es:

    This exam is not difficult.

    This boy isn’t smart.

    This beach isn’t beautiful.

    This doctor doesn’t work very well.

    This dog doesn´t eat chicken.

  • QUESTIONS

    La forma para hacer preguntas con this es:

  • Is this person poor? Yes, she is
    Is this place beautiful? No, it isn´t
    What is this? It´s a pencil
    How does this computer work? It works very well

    *al contestar es común substituir con it.

  • THIS: singular

  • Usamos that un objeto, un lugar o una persona que se encuentra lejos de nosotros. Usamos that sin hacer distinción del género masculino o femenino al igual que con this.

  • That girl is my girlfriend.

    That is a good question.

    That is not correct.

  • AFIRMATIVE

    Las oraciones afirmativas con that indican la existencia de un objeto, acción, persona o lugar que se encuentra técnicamente “lejana a nosotros”: también that se traduce como un “que” en las oraciones simples, pero no como adjetivo.

    That is my cat.

    That is right.

    That is my mother.

  • NEGATIVE

    La estructura del that en oraciones negativas es:

    THAT ball is not on the table.

    That isn’t the true.

    That house isn’t big.

    That guy doesn’t have money.

    That teacher doesn’t speak french.

  • QUESTIONS

    La forma para hacer preguntas con that es:

    What is that thing? It´s a car
    What is that sound? It´s nothing
    Who is that woman? She´s my mum
    Is that movie good? Yeah, it´s very good
    Is that boy your friend? Yes, he is

    *al contestar es común substituir con it o con el pronoun adecuado

    *El THAT es muy usado en oraciones como un conector “que” y no funciona como un Aquello/Aquella.

    Example: That’s the girl that I love (Aquella es la chica que yo amo)

  • THESE: plural

  • Usamos these para hacer referencia a objetos, lugares, acciones o personas que se encuentran cerca de nosotros. Usamos these también para masculino y femenino así como le hemos estado haciendo anteriormente. El adjetivo these es el plural de this.

  • AFIRMATIVE

    Las oraciones afirmativas con these indican la existencia de dos o más objetos, acciónes, personas o lugares que se encuentra técnicamente “muy cerca de nosotros”.

    These apples are very delicious.

    These plants are very happy.

  • NEGATIVE

    La estructura del these en oraciones negativas es:

    These houses are not new.

    These days are not special for me:

    Those children aren`t friends

    These parents aren’t good.

  • QUESTIONS

    La forma para hacer preguntas con these es:

    Are these people poor ? Yes, they are
    Are these your friends? No, they aren´t
    What are these? Are these notebooks ? They are books
    How much do these cakes cost? They cost 200 pesos
    What are these? They are vegetables

    *al contestar es común substituir con they.

  • THOSE: plural

  • Las oraciones afirmativas con those indican la existencia de dos o más objetos, acciones, personas o lugares que se encuentran “lejanos a nosotros”

    Those exams have five problems.

    Those guys are the Beatles.

  • NEGATIVE

    La estructura del those en oraciones negativas es:

    Those guys aren’t are good.

    Those horses are not fast.

    Those men aren’t Mexican.

  • QUESTIONS

    La forma para hacer preguntas con those es:

    Are those your classmates? Yes, they are
    Are those real? No, they aren´t
    What are those? They are cars.
    How do those computers work? They work fast
    What are those things? They are houses

  • Possessive Adjectives

  • Los adjetivos posesivos se utilizan para mostrar la posesión o la propiedad de algo. Los usamos cuando nos referimos a las personas, es más, en el sentido de la relación que la propiedad.

    Los adjetivos posesivos son los siguientes:

  • Subject Possessive Adjective
    I My
    You (singular) Your
    He His
    She Her
    It Its
    We Our
    You (plural) Your
    They Their

  • Son adjetivos que modifican un sustantivo (o sustantivos) con el fin de demostrar posesión.

  • Example:
    • If I have one pencil, it is my pencil
    • But if I have two pencils, they are my pencils

  • Los adjetivos posesivos coinciden solamente con los artículos que son poseídos, no reflejan la cantidad de propietarios que hay o el género del propietario.

  • My My book
    My My apples

  • El adjetivo posesivo tiene que estar de acuerdo con el poseedor y no con lo que se posee.

  • Examples

    • My car is blue.
    • His girlfriend is very friendly.
    • Our cat is black.
    • Their books are on the table.
    • Her cats are white.
    • Its colour is beautiful.
    • Their car is on the garage.

  • Posibles errores:

  • Los adjetivos posesivos son diferentes de los pronombres posesivos.

    Our- ours

    Our books are expensive. (Correct)

    Ours books are expensive. (Incorrect)

  • It´s vs its.

    Tenga cuidado de no confundir

    It´s = es una contracción de it is

    Its = es un adjetivo posesivo

  • POSSESSIVE CASE

  • Se puede formar el posesivo con la preposición "of" delante del sustantivo o con un apóstrofe y "s".

    Aumentar 's
    It is usually used for people
    Laura’s brother

  • 1.Se pone el apóstrofe ( ’ ) después del sustantivo y agregamos una "s".

  • Examples:

    • Jane’s book
    • Laura’s house
    • Johnny’s chocolates

  • 2.Cuando el sustantivo plural termina en s, agregamos solamente un apóstrofe.

  • Examples:

    • My friends’ books
    • The cats’ food
    • The dresses’ Julia

  • 3.Pero, si el sustantivo termina en s y es singular, agregamos 's

  • Examples:

    • Carlos’s books
    • Ulises’s apples
    • The actress’s pen

    En este caso para la pronunciación se pluraliza el nombre por ejemplo: Carlos’s = Carloses

  • 4.Muchas veces se omiten los sustantivos después del posesivo cuando éstos significan edificios o casas.

  • Examples:

    • We are at my grandparents'
    • He went to John's
    • They visited St. Peter's in Rome

    En este caso para la pronunciación se pluraliza el nombre por ejemplo: Carlos’s = Carloses

  • Con sustantivos múltiples:

  • 5.Si comparten algo, generalmente se agrega ’s al final de ellos.

    Examples:

    • Peter, Sandy and Robert’s nationality
    • Karla and Irma’s apartment
    • Yuri, Patty and Jacob’s books

  • 6.Si estos poseen algo en forma separada, se agrega 's a cada uno de ellos.

    Examples:

    • Peter’s, Sandy’s and Robert's nationality
    • Karla’s and Irma's apartment
    • Yuri, Patty and Jacob’s books

  • Posibles errores:

  • Cuando se utiliza el doble posesivo, siempre se debe recordar que esta estructura no se puede utilizar con objetos inanimados.

    • He is a friend of the foundation's. (Incorrect) (foundation- es un sustantivo inanimado)
    • He is a friend of the foundation. (Correct)

  • Hay que tener cuidado con los adjetivos posesivos y los pronombres posesivos.

    • Pavarotti is a friend of me. (Incorrect)
    • Pavarotti is a friend of mine. (Correct)
    • Maria Carey is a friend of our. (Incorrect)
    • Maria Carey is a friend of ours. (correct)

  • MODAL VERB CAN

  • Usamos este modal verb para hablar de habilidad:

  • I can speak Italian.

    You can jump very high.

    I can read fast.

    I can do many things
    You can go out
    We can leave the classroom now
    They can run fast
    She can speak French
    He can drive
    It can run 2 kilometers

    *puedes ver que un verbo modal se usa igual para todas las personas; aquí no se usas “s”

    * la forma del verbo después de can, también es normal.

  • Muchas veces notarás que también significa posibilidad o permiso:

  • I can do many things ability
    You can go out permission
    We can leave the classroom now permission
    They can run fast ability
    She can speak French possibility
    He can drive possibility
    My dog can run 2 kilometers ability

  • En forma negativa indica la falta de habilidad; o no permiso, o que algo es imposible:

  • We can’t say bad words in the classroom. (impossible)

    He can't sing. (no ability)

    They can’t go to the party. (no permission)

    Podemos no usar contraccion y el inglés es mas elegante y formal

    We cannot say bad words in the classroom. (impossible)

    He cannot sing. (no ability)

    They cannot go to the party. (no permission)

  • En preguntas, tenemos que empezar con el modal verb can:

  • Can you drive a motorcycle? Yes, I can.

    Can you tell me the truth? No, I can´t.

    Can she play the piano? Yes, she can.

  • Y con preguntas generales:

  • What can he do? He can play the guitar and the bass.

    How many languages can she speak? She can speak 2 languages.

  • SIMPLE PRESENT

  • Este tiempo es empleado para describir acciones que son habituales y actividades de rutina

    Students often make mistakes.

    I always get up at 7:00 a.m.

    She usually takes a shower in the morning.

    Uses

  • Repeated Actions

  • El presente simple se utiliza para expresar la idea de que una acción es repetitiva o es usual. La acción puede ser un hábito, un hobby, un evento diario o algo que pasa seguido. También puede ser algo que una persona olvida hacer o usualmente no lo hace.

    I play tennis.

    She doesn´t drink water.

    Does he eat pizza?

    The train leaves every morning at 7 a.m.

  • Repeated Actions

  • El presente simple indica que se cree en un hecho verdadero o que podría ser verdadero en el futuro. También es usado para hacer generalizaciones acerca de personas o cosas.

    Cats like milk.

    Cows do not like meal.

    Do babies like milk?

  • Scheduled Events in the Near Future

  • Es usado para hablar sobre horarios fijados, como el transporte público.

    The train leaves tonight at 9 p.m.

    The bus doesn´t arrive at 11 a.m., it arrives at 11 p.m.

    When do we board the plane?

    Expresiones de tiempo que se usan con simple present
    Every hour/ day/ week/ month/ summer/ year, usually, always, every morning/ evening / afternoon/ night, in the morning/ afternoon, etc.

  • Affirmative form

  • 1st Person 3rd Person
    I run every evening. The train leaves every morning at 8:00 a.m.
    You always watch T.V. She always forgets her wallet.
    We often do our homework. He lives in Paris.
    They drink beer every weekend.

    * En la tercera persona singular (he, she, it) se añade una “s” al verbo al conjugarlo en la forma afirmativa.

  • Negative form

  • 1st Person 3rd Person
    I don´t run in the mornings. She doesn´t play basketball.
    You don´t watch T. V. The train doesn´t leave at 9 a.m.
    We don´t play videogames. He doesn´t drink any alcohol.
    They don´t drink milk in the mornings.

    * En la forma negativa, se usa doesn´t o don´t. El verbo va en forma simple

  • Forma Interrogativa.

  • 1st Person 3rd Person
    Do I run in the mornings? Does it work?
    Do you watch T.V.? Yes, it does.
    Do we play videogames? Does she do her homework?
    Do they drink milk in the mornings? No, she doesn´t.
    Does he live in Canada?
    No, he lives in New York.

    * En preguntas se usa do o does. El verbo va en forma simple

  • Specific questions

  • En las Short Answers el verbo “to do” reemplaza al verbo empleado en la pregunta, ya sea la respuesta afirmativa o negativa. Solo usamos Yes or No.

    Do you get up at seven every day? Yes, I do.
    No, I don´t.
    Does he clean his room? Yes, he does.
    No, he doesn´t
    Do you like chocolate? Yes, he does.
    No, he doesn´t

    *No podrán usarse las short Answers cuando las preguntas contengan las Wh questions, es decir, where, what, who, which y why.

    un gerundio (infinitivo con -ing). Example: I like reading.

  • FRECUENCY ADVERBS

  • Uso

  • Los frequency adverbs se usan para indicar la frecuencia con la que una persona hace una actividad.

  • I always go to the gym.

    She usually reads a book.

  • Los frequency adverbs más usados son:

    • Always – Siempre
      • Gema always studies for her exams.
      • Henry always watches T.V.
      • Daniel always reads books.
    • Often – A menudo
      • Emily often drinks coffee.
      • Michael often does his homework.
      • Greg often makes exercise.
    • Frequently – Frecuentemente
      • William frequently goes to the gym.
      • Joseph frequently forgets his keys.
      • Lilian dances frequently.
    • Usually – Usualmente
      • I usually get up at 5 am.
      • Harry usually works on weekends.
      • Jake usually takes the bus to go with his parents.
    • Sometimes – A veces
      • Sometimes Sam goes to the park.
      • I sometimes travel to New Zealand.
      • Sometimes Sarah goes to the supermarket.
    • Seldom – Rara vez
      • Edward seldom has breakfast at home.
      • Elena seldom chats with her friends.
      • George seldom plays beisbol.
    • Hardly ever – casi nunca
      • I hardly ever play golf.
      • She hardly ever sleeps in class.
      • We hardly ever see horror movies.
    • Never – Nunca
      • I never smoke.
      • She never arrives early.
      • We never copy in exams.
    • Every day – Todos los días
      • Alan goes to school every day.
      • Ruth walks to her job every day.
      • I take a shower every day.
    • Every (Monday, Tuesday…)
      • Freddy practices soccer every Saturday.
      • Every Monday Sasha washes her clothes.
      • Sam cleans her room every Wednesday.
    • Once/ Twice a week, month, year – Una vez/ Dos veces a la semana, mes, año
      • Hannah goes to parties once a month.
      • Spencer vacuum the floor twice a week.
      • Nick travels to China three times every year.

    *Recuerda que cuando hablas en singular con he, she, it, el verbo lleva una “s” al final.

  • Posición de los Frequency Adverbs

    • Cuando solo hay un verbo principal, el frequency adverb se coloca antes del verbo principal y después del verb to be.
      • I usually get good grades.
      • Camila often goes to the mall.
      • You are usually at home.
      • I have never been in Australia.
      • Jason is always playing soccer.
      • Have you ever met someone famous?
      • Do you usually go to concerts?
    • *Usually, normally, generally, frequently, sometimes y occasionally, se pueden colocar al principio o al final de la oración para dar más énfasis.
      • Sometimes Alison has breakfast at home.
      • Alison have breakfast at home sometimes.
      • Liam drinks soda occasionally.
      • Occasionally Liam drinks soda.
    • *Often, Seldom y Rarely pueden ir al final especialmente si van predecidos de “Very” o “Quite”.
      • Michael gets drunk rarely.
      • Ashley goes shopping very often.
    • *Las expresiones de frecuencia que llevan Every, Most o Some pueden ir al principio o al final de la oración.
      • The teacher goes to school every Monday.
      • Some Tuesdays we watch movies at home.
    • *Las expresiones de frecuencia que se forman con Once, Twice… suelen ir al final de la oración.
      • I go to my English classes once a week.
      • Vanessa goes dancing twice a month.

  • Posición con el Simple Present

  • Singular Plural
    I always go to school. You often travel by train.
    He never arrives late. We frequently go to the mall.
    She usually goes to the park. They seldom have lunch at school.
    It sometimes sleeps with us. You have soccer practice twice a week.

  • Posición con el verb to be o con el present progressive:

  • Singular Plural
    I am never late You are often at your parents’ home.
    She is always well-dressed Sometimes we are at school
    He is rarely busy They are seldom drunk
    It is usually naked You are frequently at the cinema.
  • Singular Plural
    I am always reading a book You are never studying for your exams
    He is frequently talking about football We are often playing videogames
    She is usually listening to music They are sometimes doing their homework
    It is always sleeping You are seldom going to parties.
